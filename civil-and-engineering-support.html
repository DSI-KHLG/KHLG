Civil & Engineering Support

KHLG delivers Civil and Engineering Support Services that contribute to the effective planning, coordination, and implementation of resilient and sustainable infrastructure projects. Our team works alongside project owners and design professionals to strengthen project delivery through independent technical reviews, constructability analysis, regulatory coordination, and engineering assistance during construction. Although KHLG does not act as the civil or environmental engineer of record, our multidisciplinary professionals provide experienced technical insight and project support that help teams navigate complex regulatory environments, improve buildability, and maintain alignment with project objectives and compliance requirements. 

CORE CAPABILITIES

Providing Expert Technical Support to Ensure Resilient, Constructible, and Code-Compliant Infrastructure.

SITE DEVELOPMENT & GRADING SUPPORT

Review of topographic data, cut-in-fill concepts, and site layout plans to support efficient land use and constructability.

UTILITY & INFRASTRUCTURE ENGINEERING SUPPORT

Technical review and coordination support for water, sewer, and stormwater systems to confirm capacity, reliability, and regulatory compliance.

🤝

DRAINAGE, STORMWATER, & EROSION CONTROL SUPPORT

Review of hydrologic analysis, BMP selection, and erosion and sediment control measures to manage runoff and protect natural resources.

ENVIRONMENTAL IMPACT MITIGATION SUPPORT

Support in evaluating engineering measures intended to minimize impacts to soil, water, and ecological resources during construction and operations.

REGULATORY PERMITTING

Independent review of plans, specifications, and cost estimate to support bidding, permitting, and field implementation.

Engineering with Precision and Responsibility

KHLG’s civil and environmental engineering support approach helps ensure project documents and construction activities are technically sound, environmentally responsible, and aligned with local, territorial, and federal standards, while reducing risk, rework, and potential schedule impacts.
